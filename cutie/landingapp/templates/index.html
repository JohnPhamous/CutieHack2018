What is Cutie Hack?

Cutie Hack is a beginner-friendly, 12-hour hackathon. Hosted at UC Riverside, Cutie Hack is designed to help new hackers get used to the time crunch of a hackathon environment. Cutie Hack invites collegiate students to UC Riverside to collaborate and innovate. Throughout the 12 hours, participants work in teams on a project, attend workshops to learn about new technologies, and network with industry partners.

About Us

Since it's inaugural year of 2015, Cutie Hack's goal is to create a place where students can build and create to their heart's content. Founded by ACM and IEEE student members at the University of California, Riverside, the Cutie Hack committee strives to make Cutie Hack an easy entry point for new hackers to jump into the collegiate hackathons.

FAQ

Who can attend?

We welcome all undergraduate university students, from any major! If you have graduated within the past year, you are also welcome to attend.

Is Cutie Hack free?

Absolutely!

Is there free food?

Yes! Meals, refreshments, and snacks will be provided thoughout the event.

What should I bring?

Student IDs are required. Consider bringing a hacking machine, headphones, and a pillow!

Do I need a team?

Teams are not required, but are recommended. Membership is capped at 4 members. You are welcome to form teams before the hacking begins!
